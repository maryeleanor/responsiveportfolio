Mary  Eleanor  Casserly

Design | Photography | Artist | Swimmer | Triathlete

Hi! I'm Mary. An Artist, Designer, and Photographer living in San Francisco CA.

My passions for technology, art and philanthropy have contributed to my varied work history; which includes experience in art galleries, photography studios, non-profit organizations, youth hostels, record labels, event production, retail and higher education. Founder of Ela (a non-profit artisans co-op), and Art Against Cancer, I consciously seek and create work with meaning. My creative experience ranges from digital art installations and working in technical studios to designing print, web architectures, and user interactions for mobile apps.

I'm also a maker and crafter who likes antiquing and experimenting in the kitchen. I love to read, research and learn about different cultures. I've been referred to as both the protector, and nurturer; I often find myself vonlunteering, organizing, and being selected as a leader. My desire to help and ability to empathize has led to a human-centered design approach towards life.

Taking after my mother and grandfather, I began photographing sunsets and printing in basement darkrooms at an early age. I went on to study Photography and Computer Science at Bennington College. I approached attending the “college on the hill” as an artist-in-residency program. Which, for me, along with studying film history, music production, and philosophy, included printing photographs on fabric and creating reactive digital installations. After graduating, I headed west to San Francisco. Most recently I have been assisting MFA students at The Academy of Art University and studying UX design at General Assembly.

Outside of work, you can find me swimming in the bay, hiking with camera in hand, or training for triathlons.
