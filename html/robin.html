Robin | Mobile App Concept

Start Exploring, Robin will learn what you're looking for.

Working directly with an entrepreneur, I created Robin: a mobile app for Real Estate Listings. Users receive curated home listings depending on their preferences. Through surveys and interviews, I found that most users searching for real-estate were overwhelmed by search results; and had unique criteria about what they were looking for. The want to see what's out there, and not be contacted by agents until they're ready.

Robin is a fun, trustworthy agent at your fingertips.

Robin learns what users are looking for and improves the listings depending on user interaction over time. Users can treat the search results like an inbox. Save what they love to custom collections, and ‘delete’ when needed. Listings can be searched or sorted by price, commute time, or open house date. Users can save multiple searches; so they don't have to commit to one neighborhood or price. Users can compare homes features easily; and schedule their open house visits with a calendar and map.

  • Timeline:

    12 weeks

  • Research:

    surveys, interviews, card sorts and hands on usability tests

  • Artifacts Created:

  • Tools:

    Omnigraffle, Sketch, Illustrator, Balsamiq, Typeform, Invision



    • After an initial survey, I created a few different concepts for testing. I conducted user interviews with my own sketches and comparative analysis. I established user personas, use-flows and wireframes for an initial concept.

    We continued to gather data through typeform surveys and conducted card sorts to establish the most important home-features and app-features of our users. I used this data and continued usability tests as I create multiple versions of wireframes and app concepts.

    I utilized this qualitative data to complete the app on-boarding, navigation, wireframes, and a prototype.
